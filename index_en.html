Local Tours

How to Get to Capri

The most common way to reach Capri is by taking a ferry or hydrofoil, both departing from the port of Sorrento. Several companies offer ferry services between Sorrento and Capri, with frequent departures throughout the day. The journey takes about 20-30 minutes, depending on the type of ferry. It's advisable to check the schedules and book tickets in advance, especially during peak times or the tourist season.

For a more exclusive and personalized experience, you can rent a private boat from Sorrento to reach Capri. This option may be more expensive, but it offers flexibility and comfort. There are also group tours that provide the opportunity to circumnavigate the island with a small boat, allowing you to visit the coastal areas of the island, including its famous Faraglioni rocks.

Once you arrive in Capri, you can explore the island and its attractions, such as the Blue Grotto, the Gardens of Augustus, and the famous Piazzetta. Be sure to check the return schedules for ferries or hydrofoils to get back to Sorrento and plan your day on the island accordingly.

How to Get to Positano

The most convenient way to reach Positano from Sorrento is by taking the SITA bus. This service offers regular connections between the two locations along the coastal road. Buses depart from the Sorrento bus station, with a stop not far from the farmhouse, and take about 40-50 minutes to reach Positano, depending on traffic. It's a good option for those who prefer a comfortable and scenic journey along the coast.

Another option to get to Positano from Sorrento is by taking a ferry. Some maritime companies offer ferry services between Sorrento and Positano, providing spectacular views of the Amalfi Coast during the trip. This option can be faster than the bus, but ferry services might be less frequent, so it's advisable to check the schedules in advance.

Once you arrive in Positano, you can enjoy its charming beaches, picturesque alleys, and breathtaking coastal views. For a unique and scenic experience, consider hiking part or all of the Path of the Gods. This hiking trail, which starts from the hamlet of Nocelle just above Positano, offers stunning views of the Amalfi Coast as you walk along ancient paths and through woods.

How to Get to Pompeii

The most convenient way to reach Pompeii from Sorrento is by taking the Circumvesuviana train towards Naples. The Sorrento train station is connected to the Circumvesuviana network, which offers rail services between Sorrento and Naples, stopping at Pompeii. From the Piano di Sorrento station, take the train towards Naples and get off at the Pompei Scavi-Villa dei Misteri station. This train ride takes about 30-40 minutes, and once you get off, you'll be right in front of the entrance to the excavations. Be sure to check the train schedules in advance, as the frequency can vary throughout the day.

If you prefer to travel by bus, you can take a direct bus from Sorrento to Pompeii. Several companies offer this service, with regular departures throughout the day. However, keep in mind that the travel time might be longer compared to the train due to road traffic and stops along the way.

Once you arrive in Pompeii, you'll have the opportunity to explore the ancient ruins, one of the most famous archaeological sites in the world, offering a unique glimpse into the daily life of the ancient Roman city buried by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD.

How to Get to Naples

The most common and convenient way to travel from Sorrento to Naples is by taking the Circumvesuviana train. The Sorrento train station is connected to the Circumvesuviana rail network, which offers regular services between Sorrento and Naples. Simply take the train towards Naples and get off at the terminal station, Napoli Centrale (Garibaldi Station). The train journey takes about 1 hour and offers a scenic view of the coastal landscape along the way.

A fast and scenic option to reach Naples from Sorrento is by taking a hydrofoil. Some maritime companies offer hydrofoil services between Sorrento and Naples, providing a shorter travel time compared to ferries and buses. Hydrofoils are high-speed boats, and the journey usually takes about 30-40 minutes, depending on sea conditions and the maritime company. This option is ideal for those who want to save time and enjoy a panoramic view of the coast during the trip.
